{"footnote":"<\/br>\t\t","content":"<\/span>(1) When a person is charged with an offence\r\nconsisting of several particulars, a combination of some only of which constitutes a complete minor offence, and\r\nsuch combination is proved, but the remaining particulars are not proved, he may be convicted of the minor offence,\r\nthough he was not charged with it.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(2) When a person is charged with an offence and facts are proved which reduce it to a minor offence, he may\r\nbe convicted of the minor offence, although he is not charged with it.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(3) When a person is charged with an offence, he may be convicted of an attempt to commit such offence\r\nalthough the attempt is not separately charged.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) Nothing in this section shall be deemed to authorise a conviction of any minor offence where the conditions\r\nrequisite for the initiation of proceedings in respect of that minor offence have not been satisfied.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Illustrations<\/i><\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(a) A is charged, under section 407 of the Indian Penal Code (45 of 1860), with criminal breach of trust in respect of\r\nproperty entrusted to him as a carrier. He may be convicted of criminal breach of trust\r\nunder the said section 406.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(b) A is charged, under section 325 of the Indian Penal Code (45 of 1860), with causing grievous hurt. He proves that\r\nhe acted on grave and sudden provocation. He may be convicted under section 335 of that Code.<\/br>"}
