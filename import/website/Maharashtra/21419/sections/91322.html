{"footnote":"<\/br>\t\t","content":"<\/span>(1) A person who has\r\nonce been tried by a Court of competent jurisdiction for an offence and convicted or acquitted of such\r\noffence shall, while such conviction or acquittal remains in force, not be liable to be tried again for the same\r\noffence, nor on the same facts for any other offence for which a different charge from the one made against\r\nhim might have been made under sub-section (1)<\/i> of section 244, or for which he might have been convicted\r\nunder sub-section (2)<\/i> thereof.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(2) A person acquitted or convicted of any offence may be afterwards tried, with the consent of the\r\nState Government, for any distinct offence for which a separate charge might have been made against him\r\nat the former trial under sub-section (1) <\/i>of section 243.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(3) A person convicted of any offence constituted by any act causing consequences which, together\r\nwith such act, constituted a different offence from that of which he was convicted, may be afterwards tried\r\nfor such last-mentioned offence, if the consequences had not happened, or were not known to the Court to\r\nhave happened, at the time when he was convicted.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) A person acquitted or convicted of any offence constituted by any acts may, notwithstanding such\r\nacquittal or conviction, be subsequently charged with, and tried for, any other offence constituted by the\r\nsame acts which he may have committed if the Court by which he was first tried was not competent to try\r\nthe offence with which he is subsequently charged.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(5) A person discharged under section 281 shall not be tried again for the same offence except with the\r\nconsent of the Court by which he was discharged or of any other Court to which the first-mentioned Court\r\nis subordinate.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(6) Nothing in this section shall affect the provisions of section 26 of the General Clauses Act, 1897\r\n(10 of 1897) or of section 208 of this Sanhita.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Explanation.<\/i>\u2014The dismissal of a complaint, or the discharge of the accused, is not an acquittal for the\r\npurposes of this section.<\/br>

Illustrations.<\/center><\/i><\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(a) A is tried upon a charge of theft as a servant and acquitted. He cannot afterwards, while the acquittal\r\nremains in force, be charged with theft as a servant, or, upon the same facts, with theft simply, or with\r\ncriminal breach of trust.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(b) A is tried for causing grievous hurt and convicted. The person injured afterwards dies. A may be\r\ntried again for culpable homicide.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(c) A is charged before the Court of Session and convicted of the culpable homicide of B. A may not\r\nafterwards be tried on the same facts for the murder of B.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(d) A is charged by a Magistrate of the first class with, and convicted by him of, voluntarily causing\r\nhurt to B. A may not afterwards be tried for voluntarily causing grievous hurt to B on the same facts, unless\r\nthe case comes within sub-section (3) <\/i>of this section.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(e) A is charged by a Magistrate of the second class with, and convicted by him of, theft of property\r\nfrom the person of B. A may subsequently be charged with, and tried for, robbery on the same facts.<\/br>
