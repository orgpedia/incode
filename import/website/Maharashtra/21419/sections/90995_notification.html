{"footnote":"<\/br>\t\t","content":"<\/span>(1) The State Government shall establish a Court of Session for every sessions\r\ndivision.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(2) Every Court of Session shall be presided over by a Judge, to be appointed by the High Court.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(3) The High Court may also appoint Additional Sessions Judges to exercise jurisdiction in a Court of\r\nSession.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) The Sessions Judge of one sessions division may be appointed by the High Court to be also an\r\nAdditional Sessions Judge of another division, and in such case, he may sit for the disposal of cases at such\r\nplace or places in the other division as the High Court may direct.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(5) Where the office of the Sessions Judge is vacant, the High Court may make arrangements for the\r\ndisposal of any urgent application which is, or may be, made or pending before such Court of Session by\r\nan Additional Sessions Judge or if there be no Additional Sessions Judge, by a Chief Judicial Magistrate,\r\nin the sessions division; and every such Judge or Magistrate shall have jurisdiction to deal with any such\r\napplication.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(6) The Court of Session shall ordinarily hold its sitting at such place or places as the High Court may,\r\nby notification, specify; but, if, in any particular case, the Court of Session is of opinion that it will tend to\r\nthe general convenience of the parties and witnesses to hold\r\nits sittings at any other place in the sessions division, it may, with the consent of the prosecution and\r\nthe accused, sit at that place for the disposal of the case or the examination of any witness or witnesses\r\ntherein.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(7) The Sessions Judge may, from time to time, make orders consistent with this Sanhita, as to the\r\ndistribution of business among such Additional Sessions Judges.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(8) The Sessions Judge may also make provision for the disposal of any urgent application, in the event\r\nof his absence or inability to act, by an Additional Sessions Judge or if there be no Additional Sessions\r\nJudge, by the Chief Judicial Magistrate, and such Judge or Magistrate shall be deemed to have jurisdiction\r\nto deal with any such application<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Explanation.<\/i>---For the purposes of this Sanhita, appointment does not include the first appointment,\r\nposting or promotion of a person by the Government to any Service, or post in connection with the affairs\r\nof the Union or of a State, where under any law, such appointment, posting or promotion is required to be\r\nmade by the Government.\r\n<\/br>
