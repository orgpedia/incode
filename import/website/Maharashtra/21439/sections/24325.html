\r\n<\/span>\r\n2<\/sup>[(1A) Where an arbitration agreement provides for a reference of the dispute to an even number of\r\narbitrators, the agreement shall provide for the appointment of another person as umpire who shall enter\r\nupon the reference, if the arbitrators are equally divided in their opinion, and the award of the umpire\r\nshall prevail and shall be deemed to be the arbitration award for the purposes of this Act.]<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(2) An arbitration agreement referred to in sub-section (1)<\/i> shall be in such form and shall be signed\r\nby the parties thereto in such manner as may be prescribed.<\/br>

\r\n<\/span>\"10B. \r\n<\/span><\/span>(b) for requiring any public utility service not to close or remain closed and to work or continue to\r\nwork on such terms and conditions as may be specified in the order; and<\/br>

\r\nInsertion of new Chapter III-A in Central Act XIV of 1947.<\/b>- For section 10A of the principal Act,\r\nthe following new Chapter shall be inserted, namely:-<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span>\"CHAPTER III-A.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span>Arbitration.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>10B. Submission.<\/b>-(1) Any employer and a Representative Union or, in the absence of any registered\r\nRepresentative Union, any other Union which is representative of employees may, by a written\r\nagreement, agree to submit any present or future industrial dispute or class of such disputes to the\r\narbitration of any person whether such arbitrator is named in such agreement or not. Such agreement shall\r\nbe called a submission.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(2) A copy of every such submission shall be sent to the Registrar who shall register it in the register\r\nto be maintained for the purpose and shall publish it in such manner as may be prescribed.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>10C. Submission when revocable.<\/b>-Every submission shall in the absence of any provision to the\r\ncontrary contained therein be irrevocable:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Provided that a submission to refer future disputes to arbitration may at any time be revoked by any\r\nof the parties to such submission by giving the other party three months' notice in writing:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Provided further that, before the expiry of the said period of three months the parties may agree to\r\ncontinue the submission for such further period as may be agreed upon between them.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>10D. Proceedings in arbitration.<\/b>-The proceedings in arbitration under this Chapter shall be in\r\naccordance with the provisions of the Arbitration Act, 1940 (Central Act X of 1940) in so far as they are\r\napplicable and the powers which are exercisable by a Civil Court under the said provisions shall be\r\nexercisable by the Industrial Tribunal.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>10E. Special case may be stated to Industrial Tribunal.<\/b>- The arbitrator may refer any question of law\r\narising before him in any proceeding under this Act to the Industrial Tribunal for its decision. Any award\r\nmade by the arbitrator shall be in accordance with such decision.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>10F, Award by arbitrator.<\/b>-The arbitrator shall, after hearing the parties concerned, make an award\r\nwhich shall be signed by him.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>10G. Dispute to be referred to Industrial Tribunal if no arbitrator appointed.-<\/b>Notwithstanding\r\nanything contained in this Chapter, if no provision has been made in any submission for the appointment\r\nof an arbitrator or where by reason of any circumstances no arbitrator is appointed, such dispute may be\r\nreferred by the State Government for adjudication by the Industrial Tribunal.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>110H. State Government may refer industrial dispute to Industrial Tribunal for adjudication<\/b>.- \r\nNotwithstanding anything contained in this Chapter the State Government may, at any time, refer an\r\nindustrial dispute for adjudication by the Industrial Tribunal, if on a report made by the Conciliation \r\nOfficer or otherwise it is satisfied that-<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(A) by reason of the continuance of the dispute-<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span><\/span>(a) a serious outbreak of disorder or a breach of the public peace is likely to occur; or<\/br>
\r\n\r\n<\/span><\/span><\/span>(b) serious or prolonged hardship to a large section of the community is likely to be\r\ncaused; or<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span><\/span>(c) the industry concerned is likely to be seriously affected or the prospects and scope for\r\nemployment therein curtailed; or,<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(B) the dispute is not likely to be settled by other means; or<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(C) it is necessary in the public interest to do so.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(2) When the State Government makes a reference to the Industrial Tribunal for adjudication of any\r\nindustrial dispute, any submission or any award of an arbitrator with regard to that industrial dispute shall\r\nstand as cancelled.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>10I. Notice of award to parties.<\/b>- (1) The arbitrator or the Industrial Tribunal as an arbitrator, as the\r\ncase may be, shall forward copies of the award made by him or it to the parties, the Commissioner of\r\nLabour, the Registrar and the State Government.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(2) On receipt of such award, the Registrar shall enter it in the register kept for the purpose.<\/br>
