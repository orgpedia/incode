\r\n<\/span>(2) An order may be made under sub-section (1) for the delivery of any property to any person claiming to be\r\nentitled to the possession thereof, without any condition or on condition that he executes a bond, with or without\r\nsecurities, to the satisfaction of the Court, engaging to restore such property to the Court if the order made under\r\nsub-section (1) is modified or set aside on appeal or revision.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(3) A Court of Session may, instead of itself making an order under sub-section (1), direct the property to be\r\ndelivered to the Chief Judicial Magistrate, who shall thereupon deal with it in the manner provided in sections 457,\r\n458 and 459.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) Except where the property is livestock or is subject to speedy and natural decay, or where a bond has been\r\nexecuted in pursuance of sub-section (2), an order made under sub-section (1) shall not be carried out for two\r\nmonths, or when an appeal is presented, until such appeal has been disposed of.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(5) In this section, the term \"property\" includes, in the case of property regarding which an offence appears to\r\nhave been committed, not only such property as has been originally in the possession or under the control of any\r\nparty, but also any property into or for which the same may have been converted or exchanged, and anything\r\nacquired by such conversion or exchange, whether immediately or otherwise.<\/br>"}
