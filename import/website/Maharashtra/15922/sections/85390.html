\r\n<\/span>Provided that, the Court may entertain a complaint after the period of ninety days from the date of \r\nthe alleged occurrence, if good and sufficient reasons are shown by the complainant for the late filing \r\nof the complaint. <\/br>

\r\n<\/span>(3<\/i>) On receipt of a complaint under sub-section (1<\/i>), the Court may, if it so considers necessary, \r\nfirst cause an investigation into the said complaint to be made by the Investigating Officer, and direct \r\nthat a report in the matter may be submitted by him to the Court, within the period specified in the \r\ndirection. <\/br>

\r\n<\/span>(5<\/i>) The Investigating Officer shall, after investigating into the complaint under sub-section (4<\/i>) \r\nsubmit his report to the Court, within the time specified by it, setting out the full facts and \r\ncircumstances of the case, and the efforts made by him in settling the complaint. The Court shall, on \r\ndemand and on payment of such fee as may be prescribed by rules, supply a copy of the report to the \r\ncomplainant and the person complained against. <\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(6<\/i>) If, on receipt of the report of the Investigating Officer, the Court finds that the complaint has \r\nnot been settled satisfactorily, and that facts and circumstances of the case require, that the matter\r\nshould be further considered by it, the Court shall proceed to consider it, and give its decision. <\/br>
