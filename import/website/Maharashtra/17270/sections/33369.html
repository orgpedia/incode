\r\n<\/span>*<\/sup>[35. Costs.<\/b>(1) In relation to any commercial dispute, the Court, notwithstanding anything contained in\r\nany other law for the time being in force or Rule, has the discretion to determine:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(a) whether costs are payable by one party to another;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(b) the quantum of those costs; and<\/br>

\r\n<\/span><\/span>Explanation.<\/i>-- For the purpose of clause (a), the expression costs shall mean reasonable costs\r\nrelating to-- <\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n
\r\n<\/span><\/span><\/span>(i) the fees and expenses of the witnesses incurred;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n
\r\n<\/span><\/span><\/span>(ii) legal fees and expenses incurred;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n
\r\n<\/span><\/span><\/span>(iii) any other expenses incurred in connection with the proceedings.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n
\r\n<\/span>(2) If the Court decides to make an order for payment of costs, the general rule is that the unsuccessful\r\nparty shall be ordered to pay the costs of the successful party: <\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n

\r\n<\/span>\r\n
\r\n<\/span>In such circumstances the Court may impose costs on the Plaintiff, despite the Plaintiff being the successful\r\nparty, for having raised frivolous claims for damages.<\/br>
