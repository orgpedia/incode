{"footnote":"<\/br>\t\t","content":"<\/span>(1) Whoever, by deceiving any person, fraudulently or dishonestly induces the\r\nperson so deceived to deliver any property to any person, or to consent that any person shall retain any\r\nproperty, or intentionally induces the person so deceived to do or omit to do anything which he would not\r\ndo or omit if he were not so deceived, and which act or omission causes or is likely to cause damage or\r\nharm to that person in body, mind, reputation or property, is said to cheat.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Explanation.<\/i>\u2014A dishonest concealment of facts is a deception within the meaning of this section.<\/br>
\r\n
Illustrations.<\/center><\/i><\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(a) A, by falsely pretending to be in the Civil Service, intentionally deceives Z, and thus dishonestly\r\ninduces Z to let him have on credit goods for which he does not mean to pay. A cheats.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(b) A, by putting a counterfeit mark on an article, intentionally deceives Z into a belief that this\r\narticle was made by a certain celebrated manufacturer, and thus dishonestly induces Z to buy and pay for\r\nthe article. A cheats.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(c) A, by exhibiting to Z a false sample of an article intentionally deceives Z into believing that the\r\narticle corresponds with the sample, and thereby dishonestly induces Z to buy and pay for the article. A\r\ncheats.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(d) A, by tendering in payment for an article a bill on a house with which A keeps no money, and by\r\nwhich A expects that the bill will be dishonoured, intentionally deceives Z, and thereby dishonestly\r\ninduces Z to deliver the article, intending not to pay for it. A cheats.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(e) A, by pledging as diamonds articles which he knows are not diamonds, intentionally deceives Z,\r\nand thereby dishonestly induces Z to lend money. A cheats.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(f) A intentionally deceives Z into a belief that A means to repay any money that Z may lend to him\r\nand thereby dishonestly induces Z to lend him money, A not intending to repay it. A cheats.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(g) A intentionally deceives Z into a belief that A means to deliver to Z a certain quantity of indigo\r\nplant which he does not intend to deliver, and thereby dishonestly induces Z to advance money upon the\r\nfaith of such delivery. A cheats; but if A, at the time of obtaining the money, intends to deliver the indigo\r\nplant, and afterwards breaks his contract and does not deliver it, he does not cheat, but is liable only to a\r\ncivil action for breach of contract.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(h) A intentionally deceives Z into a belief that A has performed A\u2019s part of a contract made with Z,\r\nwhich he has not performed, and thereby dishonestly induces Z to pay money. A cheats.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(i) A sells and conveys an estate to B. \r\n<\/span>(2) Whoever cheats shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may\r\nextend to three years, or with fine, or with both.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(3) Whoever cheats with the knowledge that he is likely thereby to cause wrongful loss to a person\r\nwhose interest in the transaction to which the cheating relates, he was bound, either by law, or by a legal\r\ncontract, to protect, shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may\r\nextend to five years, or with fine, or with both.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) Whoever cheats and thereby dishonestly induces the person deceived to deliver any property to\r\nany person, or to make, alter or destroy the whole or any part of a valuable security, or anything which is\r\nsigned or sealed, and which is capable of being converted into a valuable security, shall be punished with\r\nimprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to seven years, and shall also be liable to\r\nfine.<\/br>
