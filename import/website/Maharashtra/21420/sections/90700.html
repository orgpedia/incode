{"footnote":"<\/br>\t\t","content":"<\/span>A person is said to make a false document or false electronic\r\nrecord---<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(A) Who dishonestly or fraudulently---<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(i) makes, signs, seals or executes a document or part of a document;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(ii) makes or transmits any electronic record or part of any electronic record;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(iii) affixes any electronic signature on any electronic record;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(iv) makes any mark denoting the execution of a document or the authenticity of the\r\nelectronic signature,<\/br>
\r\nwith the intention of causing it to be believed that such document or part of document, electronic\r\nrecord or electronic signature was made, signed, sealed, executed, transmitted or affixed by or by the\r\nauthority of a person by whom or by whose authority he knows that it was not made, signed, sealed,\r\nexecuted or affixed; or<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(B) Who without lawful authority, dishonestly or fraudulently, by cancellation or otherwise,\r\nalters a document or an electronic record in any material part thereof, after it has been made, executed\r\nor affixed with electronic signature either by himself or by any other person, whether such person be\r\nliving or dead at the time of such alteration; or<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(C) Who dishonestly or fraudulently causes any person to sign, seal, execute or alter a document\r\nor an electronic record or to affix his electronic signature on any electronic record knowing that such\r\nperson by reason of unsoundness of mind or intoxication cannot, or that by reason of deception\r\npractised upon him, he does not know the contents of the document or electronic record or the nature\r\nof the alteration.<\/br>
\r\n
Illustrations.<\/center><\/i><\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(a) A has a letter of credit upon B for rupees 10,000, written by Z. A, in order to defraud B, adds\r\ncipher to the 10,000, and makes the sum 1,00,000 intending that it may be believed by B that Z so wrote\r\nthe letter. A has committed forgery.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(b) A, without Z\u2019s authority, affixes Z\u2019s seal to a document purporting to be a conveyance of an\r\nestate from Z to A, with the intention of selling the estate to B and thereby of obtaining from B the\r\npurchase-money. A has committed forgery.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(c) A picks up a cheque on a banker signed by B, payable to bearer, but without any sum having been\r\ninserted in the cheque. A fraudulently fills up the cheque by inserting the sum of ten thousand rupees. A\r\ncommits forgery.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(d) A leaves with B, his agent, a cheque on a banker, signed by A, without inserting the sum payable\r\nand authorises B to fill up the cheque by inserting a sum not exceeding ten thousand rupees for the\r\npurpose of making certain payments. B fraudulently fills up the cheque by inserting the sum of\r\ntwenty thousand rupees. B commits forgery.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(e) A draws a bill of exchange on himself in the name of B without B\u2019s authority, intending to\r\ndiscount it as a genuine bill with a banker and intending to take up the bill on its maturity. Here, as A\r\ndraws the bill with intent to deceive the banker by leading him to suppose that he had the security of B,\r\nand thereby to discount the bill, A is guilty of forgery.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(f) Z\u2019s will contains these words\u2014\"I direct that all my remaining property be equally divided\r\nbetween A, B and C.\" A dishonestly scratches out B\u2019s name, intending that it may be believed that the\r\nwhole was left to himself and C. A has committed forgery.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(g) A endorses a Government promissory note and makes it payable to Z or his order by writing on\r\nthe bill the words \"Pay to Z or his order\" and signing the endorsement. B dishonestly erases the words\r\n\u201cPay to Z or his order\u201d, and thereby converts the special endorsement into a blank endorsement. B\r\ncommits forgery.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(h) A sells and conveys an estate to Z. A afterwards, in order to defraud Z of his estate, executes a\r\nconveyance of the same estate to B, dated six months earlier than the date of the conveyance to Z,\r\nintending it to be believed that he had conveyed the estate to B before he conveyed it to Z. A has\r\ncommitted forgery.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(i) Z dictates his will to A. A intentionally writes down a different legatee from the legatee named\r\nby Z, and by representing to Z that he has prepared the will according to his instructions, induces Z to\r\nsign the will. A has committed forgery.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(j) A writes a letter and signs it with B\u2019s name without B\u2019s authority, certifying that A is a man of\r\ngood character and in distressed circumstances from unforeseen misfortune, intending by means of such\r\nletter to obtain alms from Z and other persons. Here, as A made a false document in order to induce Z to\r\npart with property, A has committed forgery.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(k) A without B\u2019s authority writes a letter and signs it in B\u2019s name certifying to A\u2019s character,\r\nintending thereby to obtain employment under Z. A has committed forgery in as much as he intended to\r\ndeceive Z by the forged certificate, and thereby to induce Z to enter into an express or implied contract\r\nfor service.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Explanation<\/i> 1.\u2014A man\u2019s signature of his own name may amount to forgery.<\/br>
\r\n
Illustrations.<\/i><\/center><\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(a) A signs his own name to a bill of exchange, intending that it may be believed that the bill was\r\ndrawn by another person of the same name. A has committed forgery.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(b) A writes the word \"accepted\" on a piece of paper and signs it with Z\u2019s name, in order that B may\r\nafterwards write on the paper a bill of exchange drawn by B upon Z, and negotiate the bill as though it\r\nhad been accepted by Z. A is guilty of forgery; and if B, knowing the fact, draws the bill upon the paper\r\npursuant to A\u2019s intention, B is also guilty of forgery.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(c) A picks up a bill of exchange payable to the order of a different person of the same name. A\r\nendorses the bill in his own name, intending to cause it to be believed that it was endorsed by the person\r\nto whose order it was payable; here A has committed forgery.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(d) A purchases an estate sold under execution of a decree against B. B, after the seizure of the estate,\r\nin collusion with Z, executes a lease of the estate, to Z at a nominal rent and for a long period and dates\r\nthe lease six months prior to the seizure, with intent to defraud A, and to cause it to be believed that the\r\nlease was granted before the seizure. B, though he executes the lease in his own name, commits forgery\r\nby antedating it.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(e) A, a trader, in anticipation of insolvency, lodges effects with B for A\u2019s benefit, and with intent to\r\ndefraud his creditors; and in order to give a colour to the transaction, writes a promissory note binding\r\nhimself to pay to B a sum for value received, and antedates the note, intending that it may be believed to\r\nhave been made before A was on the point of insolvency. A has committed forgery under the first head of\r\nthe definition.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Explanation <\/i>2.\u2014The making of a false document in the name of a fictitious person, intending it to be\r\nbelieved that the document was made by a real person, or in the name of a deceased person, intending it\r\nto be believed that the document was made by the person in his lifetime, may amount to forgery.<\/br>
