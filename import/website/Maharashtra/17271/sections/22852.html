\r\nthe following section shall be inserted, namely:-<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>438. Direction for grant of bail to person apprehending arrest.--(1) Where any person has reason\r\nto believe that he may be arrested on accusation or having committed a non-bailable offence, he may\r\napply to the High Court or the Court of Session for a direction under this section that in the event of\r\nsuch arrest he shall be released on bail; and that Court may, after taking into consideration, inter alia,\r\nthe following factors, namely:-<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(i) the nature and gravity of the accusation;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(ii) the antecedents of the applicant including the fact as to whether he has previously undergone\r\nimprisonment on conviction by a Court in respect of any cognizable offence;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(iii) the possibility of the applicant to flee from justice; and<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(iv) where the accusation has been made with the object of injuring or humiliating the applicant\r\nby having him so arrested;<\/br>
\r\neither reject the application forthwith or issue an interim order for the grant of anticipatory bail:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Provided that where the High Court or, as the case may be, the Court of Session, has not passed\r\nany interim order under this sub-section or has rejected the application for grant of anticipatory bail, it\r\nshall be open to an officer in-charge of a police station to arrest, without warrant, the applicant on the\r\nbasis of the accusation apprehended in such application.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(2) Where the High Court or, as the case may be, the Court of Session, considers it expedient to\r\nissue an interim order to grant anticipatory bail under sub-section (1), the Court shall indicate therein\r\nthe date, on which the application for grant of anticipatory bail shall be finally heard for passing an\r\norder thereon, as the Court may deem fit, and if the Court passes any order granting anticipatory bail,\r\nsuch order shall include inter alia the following conditions, namely:-<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(i) that the applicant shall make himself available for interrogation by a police officer as and\r\nwhen required;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(ii) that the applicant shall not, directly or indirectly, make any inducement, threat or promise\r\nto any person acquainted with the facts of the case so as to dissuade him from disclosing such\r\nfacts to the Court or to any police officer;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(iii) that the applicant shall not leave India without the previous permission of the Court; and\r\n<\/span><\/span>(iv) such other conditions as may be imposed under sub-section (3) of section 437, as if the\r\nbail were granted under that section.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Explanation:-The final order made on an application for direction under sub-section (1); shall not\r\nbe construed as an interlocutory order for the purpose of this Code.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(3) Where the Court grants an interim order under sub-section (1), it shall forthwith cause a notice\r\nbeing not less than seven days notice, together with a copy of such order to be served on the Public\r\nProsecutor and the Superintendent of Police, with a view to give the Public Prosecutor a reasonable\r\nopportunity of being heard when the application shall be finally heard by the Court.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) On the date indicated in the interim order under sub-section (2), the Court shall hear the\r\nPublic Prosecutor and the applicant and after due consideration of their contentions, it may either\r\nconfirm, modify or cancel the interim order.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(5) The High Court or the Court of Session, as the case may be, shall finally dispose of an\r\napplication for grant of anticipatory bail under sub-section (1), within thirty days of the date of such\r\napplication.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(6) Provisions of this section shall not be applicable,--<\/br>

\r\n<\/span><\/span><\/span>(iv) the Uttar Pradesh Gangsters and Anti-Social Activities (Prevention) Act, 1986.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(b) in the offences, in which death sentence can be awarded.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(7) If an application under this section has been made by any person to the High Court, no\r\napplication by the same person shall be entertained by the Court of Session.<\/br>
