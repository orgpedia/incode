Conditions precedent to retrenchment of workmen.--<\/b>(1) No workman employed in any\r\nindustrial establishment to which this Chapter applies, who has been in continuous service for not less\r\nthan one year under an employer shall be retrenched by that employer until,--<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>\r\n(a) the workman has been given three months notice in writing indicating the reasons for\r\nretrenchment and the period of notice has expired, or the workman has been paid in lieu of such\r\nnotice, wages for the period of the notice; and<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>\r\n(b) the prior permission of the appropriate Government or such authority as may be specified by\r\nthat Government by notification in the Official Gazette (hereafter in this section referred to as the\r\nspecified authority) has been obtained on an application made in this behalf.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(2) An application for permission under sub-section (1)<\/i> shall be made by the employer in the\r\nprescribed manner stating clearly the reasons for the intended retrenchment and a copy of such\r\napplication shall also be served simultaneously on the workmen concerned in the prescribed manner.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(3) Where an application for permission under sub-section (1)<\/i> has been made, the appropriate\r\nGovernment or the specified authority, after making such enquiry as it thinks fit and after giving a\r\nreasonable opportunity of being heard to the employer, the workmen concerned and the persons interested\r\nin such retrenchment, may, having regard to the genuineness and adequacy of the reasons stated by the\r\nemployer, the interests of the workmen and all other relevant factors, by order and for reasons to be\r\nrecorded in writing, grant or refuse to grant such permission and a copy of such order shall be\r\ncommunicated to the employer and the workmen.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(4) Where an application for permission has been made under sub-section (1)<\/i> and the appropriate\r\nGovernment or the specified authority does not communicate the order granting or refusing to grant\r\npermission to the employer within a period of sixty days from the date on which such application is made,\r\nthe permission applied for shall be deemed to have been granted on the expiration of the said period of\r\nsixty days.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(5) An order of the appropriate Government or the specified authority granting or refusing to grant\r\npermission shall, subject to the provisions of sub-section (6)<\/i>, be final and binding on all the parties\r\nconcerned and shall remain in force for one year from the date of such order.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(6) The appropriate Government or the specified authority may, either on its own motion or on the\r\napplication made by the employer or any workman, review its order granting or refusing to grant\r\npermission under sub-section (3)<\/i> or refer the matter or, as the case may be, cause it to be referred, to a\r\nTribunal for adjudication:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\nProvided that where a reference has been made to a Tribunal under this sub-section, it shall pass an\r\naward within a period of thirty days from the date of such reference.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(7) Where no application for permission under sub-section (1)<\/i> is made, or where the permission for\r\nany retrenchment has been refused, such retrenchment shall be deemed to be illegal from the date on\r\nwhich the notice of retrenchment was given to the workman and the workman shall be entitled to all the\r\nbenefits under any law for the time being in force as if no notice had been given to him.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(8) Notwithstanding anything contained in the foregoing provisions of this section, the appropriate\r\nGovernment may, if it is satisfied that owing to such exceptional circumstances as accident in the\r\nestablishment or death of the employer or the like, it is necessary so to do, by order, direct, that the\r\nprovisions of sub- section (1)<\/i> shall not apply in relation to such establishment for such period as may be\r\nspecified in the order.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(9) Where permission for retrenchment has been granted under sub-section (3)<\/i> or where permission\r\nfor retrenchment is deemed to be granted under sub-section (4)<\/i>, every workman who is employed in that\r\nestablishment immediately before the date of application for permission under this section shall be\r\nentitled to receive, at the time of retrenchment, compensation which shall be equivalent to fifteen days'\r\naverage pay for every completed year of continuous service or any part thereof in excess of six months.]]<\/br>
