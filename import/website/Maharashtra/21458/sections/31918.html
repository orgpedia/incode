\r\n<\/span>Section 57.--<\/b>For section 57, substitute the following section, namely:--<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\"57. Procedure when the offender is not known or cannot be found.--<\/b> When the offender is not known or cannot be found the Magistrate may, if he finds that an offence has been committed, but subject to section 52B, order the property in respect of which offence has been committed, to be confiscated or forfeited together with all tools, arms, boats, carts, equipment, ropes, chains, machines, vehicles, cattle or any other article used in committing the offence, and taken charge of by the Forest officer, or to be made over to the person whom the Magistrate deems to be entitled to the same:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Provided that, no such order shall be made until the expiration of one month from the date of seizing such property or without hearing the person, if any, claiming any right thereto, and the evidence, if any, which he may produce in support of his claim.\"<\/br>
