\r\n<\/span>Provided that when the inquiry or trial relates to an offence under section 64, section 65, section 66,\r\nsection 67, section 68, section 70 or section 71 of the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023 (45 of 2023) the\r\ninquiry or trial shall be completed within a period of two months from the date of filing of the chargesheet.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(2) If the Court, after taking cognizance of an offence, or commencement of trial, finds it necessary or\r\nadvisable to postpone the commencement of, or adjourn, any inquiry or trial, it may, from time to time, for\r\nreasons to be recorded, postpone or adjourn the same on such terms as it thinks fit, for such time as it\r\nconsiders reasonable, and may by a warrant remand the accused if in custody:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Provided that no Court shall remand an accused person to custody under this section for a term\r\nexceeding fifteen days at a time:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Provided further that when witnesses are in attendance, no adjournment or postponement shall be\r\ngranted, without examining them, except for special reasons to be recorded in writing:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Provided also that no adjournment shall be granted for the purpose only of enabling the accused person\r\nto show cause against the sentence proposed to be imposed on him:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Provided also that---<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(a) no adjournment shall be granted at the request of a party, except where the circumstances are\r\nbeyond the control of that party;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(b) where the circumstances are beyond the control of a party, not more than two adjournments\r\nmay be granted by the Court after hearing the objections of the other party and for the reasons to be\r\nrecorded in writing;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(c) the fact that the advocate of a party is engaged in another Court, shall not be a ground for\r\nadjournment;<\/br>
