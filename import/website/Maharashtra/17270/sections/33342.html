\r\n<\/span>\r\n(1) where consideration of a preliminary issue framed under section 9A is pending on the date of\r\ncommencement of the Code of Civil Procedure (Maharashtra Amendment) Act, 2018 (hereinafter, in this\r\nsection, referred to as \"the Amendment Act\"), the said issue shall be deemed to be an issue framed under\r\nOrder XIV of the principal Act and shall be decided by the Court, as it deems fit, along with all other\r\nissues, at the time of final disposal of the suit itself :<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\nProvided that, the evidence, if any, led by any party or parties to the suit, on the preliminary issue so\r\nframed under section 9A, shall be considered by the Court along with evidence, if any, led on other issues\r\nin the suit, at the time of final disposal of the suit itself ;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(2) in all the cases, where a preliminary issue framed under section 9A has been decided, holding that\r\nthe Court has jurisdiction to entertain the suit, and a challenge to such decision is pending before a\r\nrevisional Court, on the date of commencement of the Amendment Act, such revisional proceedings shall\r\nstand abated : <\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\nProvided that, where a decree in such suit is appealed from any error, defect or irregularity in the order\r\nupholding jurisdiction shall be treated as one of the ground of objection in the memorandum of appeal as\r\nif it had been included in such memorandum ;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(3) in all cases, where a preliminary issue framed under section 9A has been decided, holding that the\r\nCourt has no jurisdiction to entertain the suit, and a challenge to such decision is pending before an\r\nappellate or revisional Court, on the date of commencement of the Amendment Act, such appellate or\r\nrevisional proceedings shall continue as if the Amendment Act has not been enacted and section 9A has\r\nnot been deleted :<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\nProvided that, in case the appellate or revisional Court, while partly allowing such appeal or revision,\r\nremands the matter to the trial Court for reconsideration of the preliminary issue so framed under\r\nsection 9A, upon receipt of these proceedings by the trial Court, all the provisions of the principal Act\r\nshall apply ;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(4) in all cases, where an order granting an ad-interim relief has been passed under sub-section (2) of\r\nsection 9A prior to its deletion, such order shall be deemed to be an ad-interim order made under Order\r\nXXXIX of the principal Act and the Court shall, at the time of deciding the application in which such an\r\norder is made, either confirm or vacate or modify such order.<\/br>
