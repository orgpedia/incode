\r\n<\/span>(2) When a person charged with one or more offences of criminal breach of trust or dishonest misappropriation\r\nof property as provided in sub-section (2) of section 212 or in sub-section (1) of section 219, is accused of\r\ncommitting, for the purpose of facilitating or concealing the commission of that offence or those offences, one or\r\nmore offences of falsification of accounts, he may be charged with, and tried at one trial for, every such offence.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(3) If the acts alleged constitute an offence falling within two or more separate definitions of any law in force\r\nfor the time being by which offences are defined or punished, the person accused of them may be charged with, and\r\ntried at one trial for, each of such offences.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) If several acts, of which one or more than one would by itself or themselves constitute an offence, constitute\r\nwhen combined a different offence, the person accused of them may be charged with, and tried at one trial for the\r\noffence constituted by such acts when combined, and for any offence constituted by any one, or more, of such acts.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(5) Nothing contained in this section shall affect section 71 of the Indian Penal Code (45 of 1860).<\/br>
