Constitution of State Legal Services Authority.\u2014<\/b>(1) Every State Government shall constitute a\r\nbody to be called the Legal Services Authority for the State to exercise the powers and perform the\r\nfunctions conferred on, or assigned to, a State Authority under this Act.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(2) A State Authority shall consist of\u2014<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>\r\n(a) the Chief Justice of the High Court who shall be the Patron-in-Chief;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>\r\n(b) a serving or retired Judge of the High Court, to be nominated by the Governor, in consultation\r\nwith the Chief Justice of the High Court, who shall be the Executive Chairman; and<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>\r\n(c) such number of other members, possessing such experience and qualifications as may be\r\nprescribed by the State Government, to be nominated by that Government in consultation with the\r\nChief Justice of the High Court.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(3) The State Government shall, in consultation with the Chief Justice of the High Court, appoint a\r\nperson belonging to the State Higher Judicial Service, not lower in rank than that of a District Judge, as\r\nthe Member-Secretary of the State Authority, to exercise such powers and perform such duties under the\r\nExecutive Chairman of the State Authority as may be prescribed by that Government or as may be\r\nassigned to him by the Executive Chairman of that Authority.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\nProvided that a person functioning as Secretary of a State Legal Aid and Advice Board immediately\r\nbefore the date of constitution of the State Authority may be appointed as Member-Secretary of that\r\nAuthority, even if he is not qualified to be appointed as such under this sub-section, for a period not\r\nexceeding five years.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(4) The terms of office and other conditions relating thereto, of members and the Member-Secretary\r\nof the State Authority shall be such as may be prescribed by the State Government in consultation with\r\nthe Chief Justice of the High Court.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(5) The State Authority may appoint such number of officers and other employees as may be\r\nprescribed by the State Government, in consultation with the Chief Justice of the High Court, for the\r\nefficient discharge of its functions under this Act.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(6) The officers and other employees of the State Authority shall be entitled to such salary and\r\nallowances and shall be subject to such other conditions of service as may be prescribed by the State\r\nGovernment in consultation with the Chief Justice of the High Court.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(7) The administrative expenses of the State Authority, including the salaries, allowances and\r\npensions payable to the Member-Secretary, officers and other employees of the State Authority shall be\r\ndefrayed out of the Consolidated Fund of the State.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(8) All orders and decisions of the State Authority shall be authenticated by the Member-Secretary or\r\nany other officer of the State Authority duly authorised by the Executive Chairman of the State Authority.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(9) No act or proceeding of a State Authority shall be invalid merely on the ground of the existence of\r\nany vacancy in, or any defect in the constitution of, the State Authority.]<\/br>
