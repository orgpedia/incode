\r\n<\/span>Explanation.<\/i>---For the purposes of this sub-section, \"the average daily wage\" means the average of the\r\nwoman's wages payable to her for the days on which she has worked during the period of three calendar\r\nmonths immediately preceding the date from which she absents herself on account of maternity, subject to\r\nthe minimum rate of wage fixed or revised under the Code on Wages, 2019 (29 of 2019).<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(2) No woman shall be entitled to maternity benefit unless she has actually worked in an establishment\r\nof the employer from whom she claims maternity benefit, for a period of not less than eighty days in the\r\ntwelve months immediately preceding the date of her expected delivery.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Explanation.<\/i>--- For the purposes of calculating the period under this sub-section, the days on which a\r\nwoman has actually worked in the establishment, the days for which she has been laid off or was on holidays\r\ndeclared under any law for the time being in force to be holidays with wages, during the period of twelve\r\nmonths immediately preceding the expected date of her delivery shall be taken into account.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(3) The maximum period for which any woman shall be entitled to maternity benefit shall be twentysix weeks of which not more than eight weeks shall precede the expected date of her delivery:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Provided that the maximum period entitled to maternity benefit by a woman having two or more\r\nsurviving children shall be twelve weeks of which not more than six weeks shall precede the date of her\r\nexpected delivery:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Provided further that where a woman dies during this period, the maternity benefit shall be payable\r\nonly for the days up to and including the day of her death:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Provided also that where a woman, having been delivered of a child, dies during her delivery or during\r\nthe period immediately following the date of her delivery for which she is entitled for the maternity benefit,\r\nleaving behind in either case the child, the employer shall be liable for the maternity benefit for that entire\r\nperiod but if the child also dies during the said period, then, for the days up to and including the date of the\r\ndeath of the child.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Explanation.<\/i>---For the purposes of this sub-section, \"child\" includes a stillborn child.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) A woman who legally adopts a child below the age of three months or a commissioning mother\r\nshall be entitled to maternity benefit for a period of twelve weeks from the date the child is handed over to\r\nthe adopting mother or the commissioning mother, as the case may be.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(5) In case the work assigned to a woman is of such nature that she may work from home, the employer\r\nmay allow her to do so after availing of the maternity benefit for such period and on such conditions as the\r\nemployer and the woman may mutually agree.\r\n<\/br>
