{"footnote":"<\/br>\t\t","content":"<\/span>Whoever dishonestly misappropriates or converts\r\nto his own use any movable property, shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a\r\nterm which shall not be less than six months but which may extend to two years and with fine.<\/br>
\r\n
Illustrations.<\/i><\/center><\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(a) A takes property belonging to Z out of Z\u2019s possession, in good faith believing at the time when he\r\ntakes it, that the property belongs to himself. A is not guilty of theft; but if A, after discovering his\r\nmistake, dishonestly appropriates the property to his own use, he is guilty of an offence under this\r\nsection.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(b) A, being on friendly terms with Z, goes into Z\u2019s library in Z\u2019s absence, and takes away a book\r\nwithout Z\u2019s express consent. Here, if A was under the impression that he had Z\u2019s implied consent to take\r\nthe book for the purpose of reading it, A has not committed theft. But, if A afterwards sells the book for\r\nhis own benefit, he is guilty of an offence under this section.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(c) A and B, being, joint owners of a horse. A takes the horse out of B\u2019s possession, intending to use\r\nit. Here, as A has a right to use the horse, he does not dishonestly misappropriate it. But, if A sells the\r\nhorse and appropriates the whole proceeds to his own use, he is guilty of an offence under this section.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Explanation 1.<\/i>\u2014A dishonest misappropriation for a time only is a misappropriation within the\r\nmeaning of this section.<\/br>
\r\n
Illustration.<\/i><\/center><\/br>
\r\n<\/span>A finds a Government promissory note belonging to Z, bearing a blank endorsement. A, knowing that\r\nthe note belongs to Z, pledges it with a banker as a security for a loan, intending at a future time to restore\r\nit to Z. A has committed an offence under this section.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Explanation 2.<\/i>\u2014A person who finds property not in the possession of any other person, and takes\r\nsuch property for the purpose of protecting it for, or of restoring it to, the owner, does not take or\r\nmisappropriate it dishonestly, and is not guilty of an offence; but he is guilty of the offence above\r\ndefined, if he appropriates it to his own use, when he knows or has the means of discovering the owner,\r\nor before he has used reasonable means to discover and give notice to the owner and has kept the\r\nproperty a reasonable time to enable the owner to claim it.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>What are reasonable means or what is a reasonable time in such a case, is a question of fact.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>It is not necessary that the finder should know who is the owner of the property, or that any particular\r\nperson is the owner of it; it is sufficient if, at the time of appropriating it, he does not believe it to be his\r\nown property, or in good faith believe that the real owner cannot be found.<\/br>
\r\n
Illustrations.<\/i><\/center><\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(a) A finds a rupee on the high road, not knowing to whom the rupee belongs, A picks up the rupee.\r\nHere A has not committed the offence defined in this section.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(b) A finds a letter on the road, containing a bank-note. From the direction and contents of the letter\r\nhe learns to whom the note belongs. He appropriates the note. He is guilty of an offence under this\r\nsection.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(c) A finds a cheque payable to bearer. He can form no conjecture as to the person who has lost the\r\ncheque. But the name of the person, who has drawn the cheque, appears. A knows that this person can\r\ndirect him to the person in whose favour the cheque was drawn. A appropriates the cheque without\r\nattempting to discover the owner. He is guilty of an offence under this section.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(d) A sees Z drop his purse with money in it. A picks up the purse with the intention of restoring it\r\nto Z, but afterwards appropriates it to his own use. A has committed an offence under this section.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(e) A finds a purse with money, not knowing to whom it belongs; he afterwards discovers that it\r\nbelongs to Z, and appropriates it to his own use. A is guilty of an offence under this section.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(f) A finds a valuable ring, not knowing to whom it belongs. A sells it immediately without\r\nattempting to discover the owner. A is guilty of an offence under this section.<\/br>
