{"footnote":"<\/br>\t\t","content":"<\/span>(1) Whenever an Executive Magistrate is satisfied from a report of a police officer or upon other\r\ninformation that a dispute likely to cause a breach of the peace exists concerning any land or water or the\r\nboundaries thereof, within his local jurisdiction, he shall make an order in writing, stating the grounds of\r\nhis being so satisfied, and requiring the parties concerned in such dispute to attend his Court in person or\r\nby an advocate on aspecified date and time, and to put in written statements of their respective claims as\r\nrespects the fact of actual possession of the subject of dispute.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(2) For the purposes of this section, the expression \"land or water\" includes buildings, markets,\r\nfisheries, crops or other produce of land, and the rents or profits of any such property.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(3) A copy of the order shall be served in the manner provided by this Sanhita for the service of\r\nsummons upon such person or persons as the Magistrate may direct, and at least one copy shall be published\r\nby being affixed to some conspicuous place at or near the subject of dispute.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) The Magistrate shall, without reference to the merits or the claims of any of the parties to a right to\r\npossess the subject of dispute, peruse the statements so put in, hear the parties, receive all such evidence as\r\nmay be produced by them, take such further evidence, if any, as he thinks necessary, and, if possible, decide\r\nwhether any and which of the parties was, at the date of the order made by him under sub-section (1), in\r\npossession of the subject of dispute:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Provided that if it appears to the Magistrate that any party has been forcibly and wrongfully\r\ndispossessed within two months next before the date on which the report of a police officer or other\r\ninformation was received by the Magistrate, or after that date and before the date of his order under\r\nsub-section (1), he may treat the party so dispossessed as if that party had been in possession on the date of\r\nhis order under sub-section (1).<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(5) Nothing in this section shall preclude any party so required to attend, or any other person interested,\r\nfrom showing that no such dispute as aforesaid exists or has existed; and in such case the Magistrate shall\r\ncancel his said order, and all further proceedings thereon shall be stayed, but, subject to such cancellation,\r\nthe order of the Magistrate under sub-section (1) shall be final.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(6) (a) If the Magistrate decides that one of the parties was, or should under the proviso to\r\nsub-section (4) be treated as being, in such possession of the said subject of dispute, he shall issue an order\r\ndeclaring such party to be entitled to possession thereof until evicted therefrom in due course of law, and\r\nforbidding all disturbance of such possession until such eviction; and when he proceeds under the proviso\r\nto sub-section (4), may restore to possession the party forcibly and wrongfully dispossessed;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(b) the order made under this sub-section shall be served and published in the manner laid down in subsection (3).<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(7) When any party to any such proceeding dies, the Magistrate may cause the legal representative of\r\nthe deceased party to be made a party to the proceeding and shall thereupon continue the inquiry, and if any\r\nquestion arises as to who the legal representative of a deceased party for the purposes of such proceeding\r\nis, all persons claiming to be representatives of the deceased party shall be made parties thereto.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(8) If the Magistrate is of opinion that any crop or other produce of the property, the subject of dispute\r\nin a proceeding under this section pending before him, is subject to speedy and natural decay, he may make\r\nan order for the proper custody or sale of such property, and, upon the completion of the inquiry, shall make\r\nsuch order for the disposal of such property, or the sale-proceeds thereof, as he thinks fit.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(9) The Magistrate may, if he thinks fit, at any stage of the proceedings under this section, on the\r\napplication of either party, issue a summons to any witness directing him to attend or to produce any\r\ndocument or thing.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(10) Nothing in this section shall be deemed to be in derogation of powers of the Magistrate to proceed\r\nunder section 126.<\/br>
