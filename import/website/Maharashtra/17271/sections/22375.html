{"footnote":"<\/br>\t\t","content":"<\/span>(1) The State Government shall establish a Court of Session for every sessions\r\ndivision.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(2) Every Court of Session shall be presided over by a Judge, to be appointed by the High Court.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(3) The High Court may also appoint Additional Sessions Judges and Assistant Session Judges to\r\nexercise jurisdiction in a Court of Session.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) The Sessions Judge of one sessions division may be appointed by the High Court to be also an\r\nAdditional Sessions Judge of another division, and in such case he may sit for the disposal of cases at\r\nsuch place or places in the other division as the High Court may direct.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(5) Where the office of the Sessions Judge is vacant, the High Court may make arrangements for the\r\ndisposal of any urgent application which is, or may be, made or pending before such Court of Session by\r\nan Additional or Assistant Sessions Judge, or, if there be no Additional or Assistant Sessions Judge, by a\r\nChief Judicial Magistrate, in the sessions division; and every such Judge or Magistrate shall have\r\njurisdiction to deal with any such application.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(6) The Court of Session shall ordinarily hold its sitting at such place or places as the High Court\r\nmay, by notification, specify; but, if, in any particular case, the Court of Session is of opinion that it will\r\ntend to the general convenience of the parties and witnesses to hold its sittings at any other place in the\r\nsessions division, it may, with the consent of the prosecution and the accused, sit at that place for the\r\ndisposal of the case or the examination of any witness or witnesses therein.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Explanation.<\/i>--For the purposes of this Code, appointment does not include the first appointment,\r\nposting or promotion of a person by the Government to any Service, or post in connection with the affairs of\r\nthe Union or of a State, where under any law, such appointment, posting or promotion is required to be\r\nmade by Government.<\/br>
