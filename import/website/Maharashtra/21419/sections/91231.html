{"footnote":"<\/br>\t\t","content":"<\/span>(1) When a person is charged with an\r\noffence consisting of several particulars, a combination of some only of which constitutes a complete minor\r\noffence, and such combination is proved, but the remaining particulars are not proved, he may be convicted\r\nof the minor offence, though he was not charged with it.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(2) When a person is charged with an offence and facts are proved which reduce it to a minor offence,\r\nhe may be convicted of the minor offence, although he is not charged with it.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(3) When a person is charged with an offence, he may be convicted of an attempt to commit such\r\noffence although the attempt is not separately charged.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) Nothing in this section shall be deemed to authorise a conviction of any minor offence where the\r\nconditions requisite for the initiation of proceedings in respect of that minor offence have not been satisfied.<\/br>
