{"footnote":"<\/br>\t\t","content":"<\/span>(1) Whoever with intent to cause, or knowing that he is likely to cause, wrongful\r\nloss or damage to the public or to any person, causes the destruction of any property, or any such change\r\nin any property or in the situation thereof as destroys or diminishes its value or utility, or affects it\r\ninjuriously, commits mischief.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Explanation 1.<\/i>\u2014It is not essential to the offence of mischief that the offender should intend to cause\r\nloss or damage to the owner of the property injured or destroyed. It is sufficient if he intends to cause, or\r\nknows that he is likely to cause, wrongful loss or damage to any person by injuring any property, whether\r\nit belongs to that person or not.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Explanation 2.<\/i>\u2014Mischief may be committed by an act affecting property belonging to the person\r\nwho commits the act, or to that person and others jointly.<\/br>
\r\n
Illustrations.<\/center><\/i><\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(a) A voluntarily burns a valuable security belonging to Z intending to cause wrongful loss to Z. A\r\nhas committed mischief.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(b) A introduces water into an ice-house belonging to Z and thus causes the ice to melt, intending\r\nwrongful loss to Z. A has committed mischief.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(c) A voluntarily throws into a river a ring belonging to Z, with the intention of thereby causing\r\nwrongful loss to Z. A has committed mischief.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(d) A, knowing that his effects are about to be taken in execution in order to satisfy a debt due from\r\nhim to Z, destroys those effects, with the intention of thereby preventing Z from obtaining satisfaction of\r\nthe debt, and of thus causing damage to Z. A has committed mischief.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(e) A having insured a ship, voluntarily causes the same to be cast away, with the intention of causing\r\ndamage to the underwriters. A has committed mischief.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(f) A causes a ship to be cast away, intending thereby to cause damage to Z who has lent money on\r\nbottomry on the ship. A has committed mischief.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(g) A, having joint property with Z in a horse, shoots the horse, intending thereby to cause wrongful\r\nloss to Z. A has committed mischief.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(h) A causes cattle to enter upon a field belonging to Z, intending to cause and knowing that he is\r\nlikely to cause damage to Z\u2019s crop. A has committed mischief.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(2) Whoever commits mischief shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term\r\nwhich may extend to six months, or with fine, or with both.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(3) Whoever commits mischief and thereby causes loss or damage to any property including the\r\nproperty of Government or Local Authority shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for\r\na term which may extend to one year, or with fine, or with both.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) Whoever commits mischief and thereby causes loss or damage to the amount of twenty thousand\r\nrupees and more but less than one lakh rupees shall be punished with imprisonment of either description\r\nfor a term which may extend to two years, or with fine, or with both.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(5) Whoever commits mischief and thereby causes loss or damage to the amount of one lakh rupees\r\nor upwards, shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to\r\nfive years, or with fine, or with both.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(6) Whoever commits mischief, having made preparation for causing to any person death, or hurt, or\r\nwrongful restraint, or fear of death, or of hurt, or of wrongful restraint, shall be punished with\r\nimprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to five years, and shall also be liable to\r\nfine.<\/br>
