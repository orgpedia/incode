Rights of victims and witnesses.<\/b>--(1) It shall be the duty and responsibility of the State to\r\nmake arrangements for the protection of victims, their dependents, and witnesses against any kind of\r\nintimidation or coercion or inducement or violence or threats of violence.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(2) A victim shall be treated with fairness, respect and dignity and with due regard to any special need\r\nthat arises because of the victims age or gender or educational disadvantage or poverty.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(3) A victim or his dependent shall have the right to reasonable, accurate, and timely notice of any\r\nCourt proceeding including any bail proceeding and the Special Public Prosecutor or the State\r\nGovernment shall inform the victim about any proceedings under this Act.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(4) A victim or his dependent shall have the right to apply to the Special Court or the Exclusive\r\nSpecial Court, as the case may be, to summon parties for production of any documents or material,\r\nwitnesses or examine the persons present.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(5) A victim or his dependent shall be entitled to be heard at any proceeding under this Act in respect\r\nof bail, discharge, release, parole, conviction or sentence of an accused or any connected proceedings or\r\narguments and file written submission on conviction, acquittal or sentencing.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(6) Notwithstanding anything contained in the Code of Criminal Procedure,1973 (2 of 1974), the\r\nSpecial Court or the Exclusive Special Court trying a case under this Act shall provide to a victim, his\r\ndependent, informant or witnesses--<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>\r\n(a) the complete protection to secure the ends of justice;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>\r\n(b) the travelling and maintenance expenses during investigation, inquiry and trial;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>\r\n(c) the social-economic rehabilitation during investigation, inquiry and trial; and<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>\r\n(d) relocation.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(7) The State shall inform the concerned Special Court or the Exclusive Special Court about the\r\nprotection provided to any victim or his dependent, informant or witnesses and such Court shall\r\nperiodically review the protection being offered and pass appropriate orders.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(8) Without prejudice to the generality of the provisions of sub-section (6)<\/i>, the concerned Special\r\nCourt or the Exclusive Special Court may, on an application made by a victim or his dependent,\r\ninformant or witness in any proceedings before it or by the Special Public Prosecutor in relation to such\r\nvictim, informant or witness or on its own motion, take such measures including--<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>\r\n(a) concealing the names and addresses of the witnesses in its orders or judgments or in any\r\nrecords of the case accessible to the public;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>\r\n(b) issuing directions for non-disclosure of the identity and addresses of the witnesses;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>\r\n(c) take immediate action in respect of any complaint relating to harassment of a victim,\r\ninformant or witness and on the same day, if necessary, pass appropriate orders for protection:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>\r\nProvided that inquiry or investigation into the complaint received under clause (c)<\/i> shall be tried\r\nseparately from the main case by such Court and concluded within a period of two months from the\r\ndate of receipt of the complaint:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>\r\nProvided further that where the complaint under clause (c)<\/i> is against any public servant, the Court\r\nshall restrain such public servant from interfering with the victim, informant or witness, as the case\r\nmay be, in any matter related or unrelated to the pending case, except with the permission of the\r\nCourt.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(9) It shall be the duty of the Investigating Officer and the Station House Officer to record the\r\ncomplaint of victim, informant or witnesses against any kind of intimidation, coercion or inducement or\r\nviolence or threats of violence, whether given orally or in writing, and a photocopy of the First\r\nInformation Report shall be immediately given to them at free of cost.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(10) All proceedings relating to offences under this Act shall be video recorded.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(11) It shall be the duty of the concerned State to specify an appropriate scheme to ensure\r\nimplementation of the following rights and entitlements of victims and witnesses in accessing justice so\r\nas--<\/br>
