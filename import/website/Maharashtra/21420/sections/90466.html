{"footnote":"<\/br>\t\t","content":"<\/span>Except in the cases hereinafter excepted, culpable homicide is murder,---<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(a) if the act by which the death is caused is done with the intention of causing death; or<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(b) if the act by which the death is caused is done with the intention of causing such bodily injury\r\nas the offender knows to be likely to cause the death of the person to whom the harm is caused; or<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(c) if the act by which the death is caused is done with the intention of causing bodily injury to\r\nany person and the bodily injury intended to be inflicted is sufficient in the ordinary course of nature\r\nto cause death; or<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(d) if the person committing the act by which the death is caused, knows that it is so imminently\r\ndangerous that it must, in all probability, cause death, or such bodily injury as is likely to cause death,\r\nand commits such act without any excuse for incurring the risk of causing death or such injury as\r\naforesaid.<\/br>
\r\n
Illustrations.<\/center><\/i><\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(a) A shoots Z with the intention of killing him. Z dies in consequence. A commits murder.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(b) A, knowing that Z is labouring under such a disease that a blow is likely to cause his death, strikes\r\nhim with the intention of causing bodily injury. Z dies in consequence of the blow. A is guilty of murder,\r\nalthough the blow might not have been sufficient in the ordinary course of nature to cause the death of a\r\nperson in a sound state of health. But if A, not knowing that Z is labouring under any disease, gives him\r\nsuch a blow as would not in the ordinary course of nature kill a person in a sound state of health, here A,\r\nalthough he may intend to cause bodily injury, is not guilty of murder, if he did not intend to cause death,\r\nor such bodily injury as in the ordinary course of nature would cause death.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(c) A intentionally gives Z a sword-cut or club-wound sufficient to cause the death of a man in the\r\nordinary course of nature. Z dies in consequence. Here A is guilty of murder, although he may not have\r\nintended to cause Z's death.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(d) A without any excuse fires a loaded cannon into a crowd of persons and kills one of them. A is\r\nguilty of murder, although he may not have had a premeditated design to kill any particular individual.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Exception 1.<\/i>---Culpable homicide is not murder if the offender, whilst deprived of the power of\r\nself-control by grave and sudden provocation, causes the death of the person who gave the provocation or\r\ncauses the death of any other person by mistake or accident:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Provided that the provocation is not,---<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(a) sought or voluntarily provoked by the offender as an excuse for killing or doing harm to any\r\nperson;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(b) given by anything done in obedience to the law, or by a public servant in the lawful exercise\r\nof the powers of such public servant;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(c) given by anything done in the lawful exercise of the right of private defence.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>Explanation<\/i>.---Whether the provocation was grave and sudden enough to prevent the offence\r\nfrom amounting to murder is a question of fact.<\/br>
\r\n
Illustrations.<\/center><\/i><\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(a) A, under the influence of passion excited by a provocation given by Z, intentionally kills Y,\r\nZ's child. This is murder, in as much as the provocation was not given by the child, and the death of\r\nthe child was not caused by accident or misfortune in doing an act caused by the provocation.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(b) Y gives grave and sudden provocation to A. A, on this provocation, fires a pistol at Y, neither\r\nintending nor knowing himself to be likely to kill Z, who is near him, but out of sight. A kills Z. Here\r\nA has not committed murder, but merely culpable homicide.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(c) A is lawfully arrested by Z, a bailiff. A is excited to sudden and violent passion by the arrest,\r\nand kills Z. This is murder, in as much as the provocation was given by a thing done by a public\r\nservant in the exercise of his powers.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(d) A appears as a witness before Z, a Magistrate. Z says that he does not believe a word of A's\r\ndeposition, and that A has perjured himself. A is moved to sudden passion by these words, and kills Z.\r\nThis is murder.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(e) A attempts to pull Zs nose. Z, in the exercise of the right of private defence, lays hold of A to\r\nprevent him from doing so. A is moved to sudden and violent passion in consequence, and kills Z.\r\nThis is murder, in as much as the provocation was giving by a thing done in the exercise of the right of\r\nprivate defence.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(f) Z strikes B. B is by this provocation excited to violent rage. A, a bystander, intending to take\r\nadvantage of B's rage, and to cause him to kill Z, puts a knife into B's hand for that purpose. B kills Z\r\nwith the knife. Here B may have committed only culpable homicide, but A is guilty of murder.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Exception <\/i>2.---Culpable homicide is not murder if the offender in the exercise in good faith of the\r\nright of private defence of person or property, exceeds the power given to him by law and causes the\r\ndeath of the person against whom he is exercising such right of defence without premeditation, and\r\nwithout any intention of doing more harm than is necessary for the purpose of such defence.<\/br>
\r\n
Illustration.<\/center><\/i><\/br>
\r\nZ attempts to horsewhip A, not in such a manner as to cause grievous hurt to A. A draws out a pistol.\r\nZ persists in the assault. A believing in good faith that he can by no other means prevent himself from\r\nbeing horsewhipped, shoots Z dead. A has not committed murder, but only culpable homicide.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Exception 3.---Culpable homicide is not murder if the offender, being a public servant or aiding a\r\npublic servant acting for the advancement of public justice, exceeds the powers given to him by law, and\r\ncauses death by doing an act which he, in good faith, believes to be lawful and necessary for the due\r\ndischarge of his duty as such public servant and without ill-will towards the person whose death is\r\ncaused.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Exception 4.---Culpable homicide is not murder if it is committed without premeditation in a sudden\r\nfight in the heat of passion upon a sudden quarrel and without the offenders having taken undue\r\nadvantage or acted in a cruel or unusual manner.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Explanation.---It is immaterial in such cases which party offers the provocation or commits the first\r\nassault.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Exception 5.---Culpable homicide is not murder when the person whose death is caused, being above\r\nthe age of eighteen years, suffers death or takes the risk of death with his own consent.<\/br>

\r\nA, by instigation, voluntarily causes Z, a child to commit suicide. Here, on account of Z's youth, he\r\nwas incapable of giving consent to his own death; A has therefore abetted murder.<\/br>
