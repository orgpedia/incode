{"footnote":"<\/br>\t\t","content":"<\/span>(1) Whoever, being in any manner entrusted with property, or with\r\nany dominion over property, dishonestly misappropriates or converts to his own use that property, or\r\ndishonestly uses or disposes of that property in violation of any direction of law prescribing the mode in\r\nwhich such trust is to be discharged, or of any legal contract, express or implied, which he has made\r\ntouching the discharge of such trust, or wilfully suffers any other person so to do, commits criminal\r\nbreach of trust.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Explanation 1.<\/i>\u2014A person, being an employer of an establishment whether exempted under\r\nsection 17 of the Employees\u2019 Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952 (19 of 1952) or\r\nnot who deducts the employee\u2019s contribution from the wages payable to the employee for credit to a\r\nProvident Fund or Family Pension Fund established by any law for the time being in force, shall be\r\ndeemed to have been entrusted with the amount of the contribution so deducted by him and if he makes\r\ndefault in the payment of such contribution to the said Fund in violation of the said law, shall be deemed\r\nto have dishonestly used the amount of the said contribution in violation of a direction of law as\r\naforesaid.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Explanation 2.<\/i>\u2014A person, being an employer, who deducts the employees\u2019 contribution from the\r\nwages payable to the employee for credit to the Employees\u2019 State Insurance Fund held and administered\r\nby the Employees\u2019 State Insurance Corporation established under the Employees\u2019 State Insurance\r\nAct, 1948 (34 of 1948) shall be deemed to have been entrusted with the amount of the contribution so\r\ndeducted by him and if he makes default in the payment of such contribution to the said Fund in violation\r\nof the said Act, shall be deemed to have dishonestly used the amount of the said contribution in violation\r\nof a direction of law as aforesaid.<\/br>

Illustrations.<\/center><\/i><\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(a) A, being executor to the will of a deceased person, dishonestly disobeys the law which directs\r\nhim to divide the effects according to the will, and appropriates them to his own use. A has committed\r\ncriminal breach of trust.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(b) A is a warehouse-keeper Z going on a journey, entrusts his furniture to A, under a contract that it\r\nshall be returned on payment of a stipulated sum for warehouse room. A dishonestly sells the goods. A\r\nhas committed criminal breach of trust.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(c) A, residing in Kolkata, is agent for Z, residing at Delhi. There is an express or implied contract\r\nbetween A and Z, that all sums remitted by Z to A shall be invested by A, according to Z\u2019s direction. Z\r\nremits one lakh of rupees to A, with directions to A to invest the same in Company\u2019s paper. A dishonestly\r\ndisobeys the directions and employs the money in his own business. A has committed criminal breach of\r\ntrust.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(d) But if A, in illustration (c), not dishonestly but in good faith, believing that it will be more for Z\u2019s\r\nadvantage to hold shares in the Bank of Bengal, disobeys Z\u2019s directions, and buys shares in the Bank of\r\nBengal, for Z, instead of buying Company\u2019s paper, here, though Z should suffer loss, and should be\r\nentitled to bring a civil action against A, on account of that loss, yet A, not having acted dishonestly, has\r\nnot committed criminal breach of trust.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(e) A, a revenue-officer, is entrusted with public money and is either directed by law, or bound by a\r\ncontract, express or implied, with the Government, to pay into a certain treasury all the public money\r\nwhich he holds. A dishonestly appropriates the money. A has committed criminal breach of trust.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(f) A, a carrier, is entrusted by Z with property to be carried by land or by water. A dishonestly\r\nmisappropriates the property. A has committed criminal breach of trust.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(2) Whoever commits criminal breach of trust shall be punished with imprisonment of either\r\ndescription for a term which may extend to five years, or with fine, or with both.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(3) Whoever, being entrusted with property as a carrier, wharfinger or warehouse-keeper, commits\r\ncriminal breach of trust in respect of such property, shall be punished with imprisonment of either\r\ndescription for a term which may extend to seven years, and shall also be liable to fine.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) Whoever, being a clerk or servant or employed as a clerk or servant, and being in any manner\r\nentrusted in such capacity with property, or with any dominion over property, commits criminal breach of\r\ntrust in respect of that property, shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term\r\nwhich may extend to seven years, and shall also be liable to fine.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(5) Whoever, being in any manner entrusted with property, or with any dominion over property in his\r\ncapacity of a public servant or in the way of his business as a banker, merchant, factor, broker, attorney or\r\nagent commits criminal breach of trust in respect of that property, shall be punished with imprisonment\r\nfor life, or with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to ten years, and shall\r\nalso be liable to fine.<\/br>
