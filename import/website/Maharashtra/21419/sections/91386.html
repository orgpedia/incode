\r\n<\/span>(2) Where proceedings are submitted to a Magistrate of the first class as provided by sub-section (1) <\/i>,\r\nsuch Magistrate may thereupon pass such sentence or make such order as he might have passed or made if\r\nthe case had originally been heard by him, and, if he thinks further inquiry or additional evidence on any\r\npoint to be necessary, he may make such inquiry or take such evidence himself or direct such inquiry or\r\nevidence to be made or taken.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(3) In any case in which a person is convicted of theft, theft in a building, dishonest misappropriation,\r\ncheating or any offence under the Bharatiya Nyaya Sanhita, 2023, punishable with not more than two years\u2019\r\nimprisonment or any offence punishable with fine only and no previous conviction is proved against him,\r\nthe Court before which he is so convicted may, if it thinks fit, having regard to the age, character,\r\nantecedents or physical or mental condition of the offender and to the trivial nature of the offence or any\r\nextenuating circumstances under which the offence was committed, instead of sentencing him to any\r\npunishment, release him after due admonition.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) An order under this section may be made by any Appellate Court or by the High Court or Court of\r\nSession when exercising its powers of revision.<\/br>
