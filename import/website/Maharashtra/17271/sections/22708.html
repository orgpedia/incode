{"footnote":"<\/br>\t\t","content":"<\/span>(1) A person who has once been\r\ntried by a Court of competent jurisdiction for an offence and convicted or acquitted of such offence shall, while such\r\nconviction or acquittal remains in force, not be liable to be tried again for the same offence, nor on the same facts for\r\nany other offence for which a different charge from the one made against him might have been made under\r\nsub-section (1) of section 221, or for which he might have been convicted under sub-section (2) thereof.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(2) A person acquitted or convicted of any offence may be afterwards tried, with the consent of the State\r\nGovernment, for any distinct offence for which a separate charge might have been made against him at the former\r\ntrial under sub-section (1) of section 220.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(3) A person convicted of any offence constituted by any act causing consequences which, together with such\r\nact, constituted a different offence from that of which he was convicted, may be afterwards tried for such last\r\nmentioned offence, if the consequences had not happened, or were not known to the Court to have happened, at the\r\ntime when he was convicted.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) A person acquitted convicted of any offence constituted by any acts may, notwithstanding such acquittal or\r\nconviction, be subsequently charged with, and tried for, any other offence constituted by the same acts which he\r\nmay have committed if the Court by which he was first tried was not competent to try the offence with which he is\r\nsubsequently charged.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(5) A person discharged under section 258 shall not be tried again for the same offence except with the consent\r\nof the Court by which he was discharged or of any other Court to which the first mentioned Court is subordinate.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(6) Nothing in this section shall affect the provisions of section 26 of the General Clauses\r\nAct, 1897 (10 of 1897) or of section 188 of this Code.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Explanation<\/i>.---The dismissal of a complaint, or the discharge of the accused, is not an acquittal for the purposes\r\nof this section.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Illustrations<\/i><\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(a) A is tried upon a charge of theft as a servant and aquitted. He cannot afterwards, while the acquittal remains in\r\nforce, be charged with theft as a servant, or, upon the same facts, with theft simply, or with criminal breach of trust.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(b) A is tried for causing grievous hurt and convicted. The person injured afterwards dies. A may be tried again for\r\nculpable homicide.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(c) A is charged before the Court of Session and convicted of the culpable homicide of B. A may not afterwards be\r\ntried on the same facts for the murder of B.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(d) A is charged by a Magistrate of the first class with, and convicted by him of, voluntarily causing hurt to B. A may\r\nnot afterwards be tried for voluntarily causing grievous hurt to B on the same facts, unless the case comes within\r\nsub-section (3) of this section.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(e) A is charged by a Magistrate of the second class with, and convicted by him of, theft of property from the person of\r\nB. A may subsequently be charged with, and tried for, robbery on the same facts.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(f) A, B and C are charged by a Magistrate of the first class with, and convicted by him of, robbing D. A, B and C may\r\nafterwards be charged with, and tried for, dacoity on the same facts.\r\n<\/br>"}
