\r\n<\/span>(2) Where proceedings are submitted to a Magistrate of the first class as provided by sub-section (1), such Magistrate\r\nmay thereupon pass such sentence or make such order as he might have passed or made if the case had originally been\r\nheard by him, and, if he thinks further inquiry or additional evidence on any point to be necessary, he may make such\r\ninquiry or take such evidence himself or direct such inquiry or evidence to be made or taken.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(3) In any case in which a person is convicted of theft, theft in a building, dishonest misappropriation, cheating\r\nor any offence under the Indian Penal Code (45 of 1860), punishable with not more than two years, imprisonment or\r\nany offence punishable with fine only and no previous conviction is proved against him, the Court before which he\r\nis so convicted may, if it thinks fit, having regard to the age, character, antecedents or physical or mental condition\r\nof the offender and to the trivial nature of the offence or any extenuating circumstances under which the offence was\r\ncommitted, instead of sentencing him to any punishment, release him after due admonition.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) An order under this section may be made by any Appellate Court or by the High Court or Court of Session\r\nwhen exercising its powers of revision.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(5) When an order has been made under this section in respect of any offender, the High Court or Court of\r\nSession may, on appeal when there is a right of appeal to such Court, or when exercising its powers of revision, set\r\naside such order, and in lieu thereof pass sentence on such offender according to law:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Provided that the High Court or Court of Session shall not under this sub-section inflict a greater punishment\r\nthan might have been inflicted by the Court by which the offender was convicted.<\/br>
