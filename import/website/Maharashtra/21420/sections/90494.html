\r\n<\/span>(a) Z is sitting in a moored boat on a river. A unfastens the moorings, and thus intentionally causes\r\nthe boat to drift down the stream. Here A intentionally causes motion to Z, and he does this by disposing\r\nsubstances in such a manner that the motion is produced without any other action on any person\u2019s part. A\r\nhas therefore intentionally used force to Z; and if he has done so without Z\u2019s consent, in order to the\r\ncommitting of any offence, or intending or knowing it to be likely that this use of force will cause injury,\r\nfear or annoyance to Z, A has used criminal force to Z.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(b) Z is riding in a chariot. A lashes Z\u2019s horses, and thereby causes them to quicken their pace. Here\r\nA has caused change of motion to Z by inducing the animals to change their motion. A has therefore used\r\nforce to Z; and if A has done this without Z\u2019s consent, intending or knowing it to be likely that he may\r\nthereby injure, frighten or annoy Z, A has used criminal force to Z.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(c) Z is riding in a palanquin. A, intending to rob Z, seizes the pole and stops the palanquin. Here A\r\nhas caused cessation of motion to Z, and he has done this by his own bodily power. A has therefore used\r\nforce to Z; and as A has acted thus intentionally, without Z\u2019s consent, in order to the commission of an\r\noffence. A has used criminal force to Z.<\/br>
