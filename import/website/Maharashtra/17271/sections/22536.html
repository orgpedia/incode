\r\n<\/span>(3) If it appears to such Magistrate that such rights exist, he may make an order prohibiting any interference\r\nwith the exercise of such right, including, in a proper case, an order for the removal of any obstruction in the\r\nexercise of any such right:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Provided that no such order shall be made where the right is exercisable at all times of the year, unless such\r\nright has been exercised within three months next before the receipt under sub-section (1) of the report of a police\r\nofficer or other information leading to the institution of the inquiry, or where the right is exercisable only at\r\nparticular seasons or on particular occasions, unless the right has been exercised during the last of such seasons or\r\non the last of such occasions before such receipt.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) When in any proceedings commenced under sub-section (1) of section 145 the Magistrate finds that the\r\ndispute is as regards an alleged right of user of land or water, he may, after recording his reasons, continue with the\r\nproceedings as if they had been commenced under sub-section (1);<\/br>
