\r\n<\/span>(2) For every completed year of service or part thereof in excess of six months, the employer shall pay\r\ngratuity to an employee at the rate of fifteen days' wages or such number of days as may be notified by the\r\nCentral Government, based on the rate of wages last drawn by the employee concerned:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Provided that in the case of a piece-rated employee, daily wages shall be computed on the average of\r\nthe total wages received by him for a period of three months immediately preceding the termination of his\r\nemployment, and, for this purpose, the wages paid for any overtime work shall not be taken into account:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Provided further that in the case of an employee who is employed in a seasonal establishment and who\r\nis not so employed throughout the year, the employer shall pay the gratuity at the rate of seven days' wages\r\nfor each season:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Provided also that in the case of an employee employed on fixed term employment or a deceased\r\nemployee, the employer shall pay gratuity on pro rata<\/i> basis.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(3) The amount of gratuity payable to an employee shall not exceed such amount as may be notified by\r\nthe Central Government.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) For the purpose of computing the gratuity payable to an employee who is employed, after his\r\ndisablement, on reduced wages, his wages for the period preceding his disablement shall be taken to be the\r\nwages received by him during that period, and his wages for the period subsequent to his disablement shall\r\nbe taken to be the wages as so reduced.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(5) Nothing in this section shall affect the right of an employee to receive better terms of gratuity under\r\nany award or agreement or contract with the employer.<\/br>
