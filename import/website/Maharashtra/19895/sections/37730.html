\r\n<\/span>30A. Acquisition of land by the State Government by entering into agreement.:--<\/b>(1)\r\nNotwithstanding anything contained in the Principal Act, or any other law, whenever it appears to the\r\nState Government that the land is needed in any area for any public purpose, the State Government or\r\nits Authorized Officer will enter into an agreement with the willing land owner to sell the land in\r\nfavour of the State for the matters specified therein in a prescribed form.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(2) The State Government or its Authorized Officer shall pass an order in terms of agreement under\r\nsub-section (1) for acquisition, and the substance of the order shall be notified in the Gazette. On such\r\npublication of notification, the title, ownership and all interests of the land owner who enters into\r\nagreements, shall vest with the State, free from all encumbrances.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(3) Notwithstanding anything contained in the Registration Act, 1908 (Act l6 of 1908), no\r\nagreement entered under sub-section (1) shall be liable to registration under that Act.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) If any family, other than the family of the land owner who entered into an agreement, is\r\naffected by the acquisition of land under this section, the State Government shall pay a lumpsum\r\namount towards rehabilitation and resettlement, if any, as prescribed in the rules framed hereunder:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Provided that no agreement or the lumpsum amount towards rehabilitation and resettlement as may\r\nbe prescribed, shall be abnormally at variance to the disadvantage of the land owners.]<\/br>
