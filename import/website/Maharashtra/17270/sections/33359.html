Power of Supreme Court to transfer suits, etc.<\/b>--(1) On the application of a party, and after notice\r\nto the parties, and after hearing such of them as desire to be heard, the Supreme Court may, at any stage, if\r\nsatisfied that an order under this section is expedient for the ends of justice, direct that any suit, appeal or other\r\nproceeding be transferred from a High Court or other Civil Court in one State to a High Court or other Civil\r\nCourt in another State.<\/br>\r\n

\r\n<\/span>(3) The Court to which such suit, appeal or other proceeding is transferred shall, subject to any special\r\ndirections in the order of transfer, either retry it or proceed from the stage at which it was transferred to it.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) In dismissing any application under this section, the Supreme Court may, if it is of opinion that\r\nthe application was frivolous or vexatious, order the applicant to pay by way of compensation to any\r\nperson who has opposed the application such sum, not exceeding two thousand rupees, as it considers\r\nappropriate in the circumstances of the case.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(5) The law applicable to any suit, appeal or other proceeding transferred under this section shall be\r\nthe law which the Court in which the suit, appeal or other proceeding was originally instituted ought to\r\nhave applied to such suit, appeal or proceeding.] <\/br>"}
