\r\n<\/span>Explanation.\u2014A person is not said voluntarily to cause grievous hurt except when he both causes\r\ngrievous hurt and intends or knows himself to be likely to cause grievous hurt. But he is said voluntarily\r\nto cause grievous hurt, if intending or knowing himself to be likely to cause grievous hurt of one kind, he\r\nactually causes grievous hurt of another kind.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span>Illustration.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>A, intending of knowing himself to be likely permanently to disfigure Z\u2019s face, gives Z a blow which\r\ndoes not permanently disfigure Z\u2019s face, but which causes Z to suffer severe bodily pain for the space of\r\nfifteen days. A has voluntarily caused grievous hurt.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(2) Whoever, except in the case provided for by sub-section (2) of section 122, voluntarily causes\r\ngrievous hurt, shall be punished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to\r\nseven years, and shall also be liable to fine.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(3) Whoever commits an offence under sub-section (1) and in the course of such commission causes\r\nany hurt to a person which causes that person to be in permanent disability or in persistent vegetative\r\nstate, shall be punished with rigorous imprisonment for a term which shall not be less than ten years but\r\nwhich may extend to imprisonment for life, which shall mean imprisonment for the remainder of that\r\nperson\u2019s natural life.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) When a group of five or more persons acting in concert, causes grievous hurt to a person on the\r\nground of his race, caste or community, sex, place of birth, language, personal belief or any other similar\r\nground, each member of such group shall be guilty of the offence of causing grievous hurt, and shall be\r\npunished with imprisonment of either description for a term which may extend to seven years, and shall\r\nalso be liable to fine.\r\n<\/br>
