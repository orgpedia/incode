{"footnote":"<\/br>\t\t","content":"<\/span>(1) Every charge under this Code shall state the offence with which the\r\naccused is charged.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(2) If the law which creates the offence gives it any specific name, the offence may be described in\r\nthe charge by that name only.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(3) If the law which creates the offence does not give it any specific name, so much of the definition\r\nof the offence must be stated as to give the accused notice of the matter with which he is charged.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) The law and section of the law against which the offence is said to have been committed shall be\r\nmentioned in the charge.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(5) The fact that the charge is made is equivalent to a statement that every legal condition required by law to\r\nconstitute the offence charged was fulfilled in the particular case.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(6) The charge shall be written in the language of the Court.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(7) If the accused, having been previously convicted of any offence, is liable, by reason of such previous\r\nconviction, to enhanced punishment, or to punishment of a different kind, for a subsequent offence, and it is\r\nintended to prove such previous conviction for the purpose of affecting the punishment which the Court may think\r\nfit to award for the subsequent offence, the fact, date and place of the previous conviction shall be stated in the\r\ncharge; and if such statement has been omitted, the Court may add it at any time before sentence is passed.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Illustrations<\/i><\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(a) A is charged with the murder of B. This is equivalent to a statement that A's act fell within the definition of murder\r\ngiven in sections 299 and 300 of the Indian Penal Code (45 of 1860); that it did not fall within any of the general exceptions\r\nof the said Code; and that it did not fall within any of the five exceptions to section 300, or that, if it did fall within\r\nException 1, one or other of the three provisos to that exception applied to it.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(b) A is charged under section 326 of the Indian Penal Code (45 of 1860), with voluntarily causing grievous hurt to B\r\nby means of an instrument for shooting. This is equivalent to a statement that the case was not provided for by section 335\r\nof the said Code, and that the general exceptions did not apply to it.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(c) A is accused of murder, cheating, theft, extortion, adultery or criminal intimidation, or using a false property-mark.\r\nThe charge may state that A committed murder, or cheating, or theft, or extortion, or adultery, or criminal intimidation, or\r\nthat he used a false property-mark, without reference to the definitions, of those crimes contained in the Indian Penal Code\r\n(45 of 1860); but the sections under which the offence is punishable must, in each instance be referred to in the charge.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(d) A is charged under section 184 of the Indian Penal Code (45 of 1860) with intentionally obstructing a sale of\r\nproperty offered for sale by the lawful authority of a public servant. The charge should be in those words.\r\n<\/br>"}
