Power of court to engage experts.<\/b>-- (1) Without prejudice to the generality of the provisions\r\ncontained in the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (5 of 1908), in any suit under this Act, where the court\r\nconsiders it necessary to get expert opinion to assist it on any specific issue involved in the suit, it may\r\nengage one or more experts and direct to report to it on such issue and may secure attendance of the\r\nexpert for providing evidence, including production of documents on the issue.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(2) The court may require or direct any person to give relevant information to the expert or to\r\nproduce, or to provide access to, any relevant documents, goods or other property for his inspection.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(3) The opinion or report given by the expert shall form part of the record of the suit; and the court, or\r\nwith the permission of the court any of the parties to the suit, may examine the expert personally in open\r\ncourt on any of the matters referred to him or mentioned in his opinion or report, or as to his opinion or\r\nreport, or as to the manner in which he has made the inspection.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) The expert shall be entitled to such fee, cost or expense as the court may fix, which shall be\r\npayable by the parties in such proportion, and at such time, as the court may direct.]
