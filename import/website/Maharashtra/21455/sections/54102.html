\r\n<\/span><\/span>(a) where the employee has, during a continuous period of not less than twelve months immediately\r\npreceding the accident, been in the service of the employer who is liable to pay compensation, the\r\nmonthly wages of the employee shall be one-twelfth of the total wages which have fallen due for\r\npayment to him by the employer in the last twelve months of that period;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(b) where the whole of the continuous period of service immediately preceding the accident during\r\nwhich the employee was in the service of the employer who is liable to pay the compensation was less\r\nthan one month, the monthly wages of the employee shall be the average monthly amount which, during\r\nthe twelve months immediately preceding the accident, was being earned by an employee employed on\r\nthe same work by the same employer, or, if there was no employee so employed, by an employee\r\nemployed on similar work in the same locality;<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(c) in other cases including cases in which it is not possible for want of necessary information to\r\ncalculate the monthly wages under clause (b), the monthly wages shall be thirty times the total wages\r\nearned in respect of the last continuous period of service immediately preceding the accident from the\r\nemployer who is liable to pay compensation, divided by the number of days comprising such period.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Explanation.<\/i>---For the purposes of this section, \"a period of service\" shall be deemed to be continuous\r\nwhich has not been interrupted by a period of absence from work exceeding fourteen days.<\/br>
