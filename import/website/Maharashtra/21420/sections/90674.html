{"footnote":"<\/br>\t\t","content":"<\/span>(1) In all robbery there is either theft or extortion.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(2) Theft is robbery if, in order to the committing of the theft, or in committing the theft, or in\r\ncarrying away or attempting to carry away property obtained by the theft, the offender, for that end\r\nvoluntarily causes or attempts to cause to any person death or hurt or wrongful restraint, or fear of instant\r\ndeath or of instant hurt, or of instant wrongful restraint.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(3) Extortion is robbery if the offender, at the time of committing the extortion, is in the presence of\r\nthe person put in fear, and commits the extortion by putting that person in fear of instant death, of instant\r\nhurt, or of instant wrongful restraint to that person or to some other person, and, by so putting in fear,\r\ninduces the person so put in fear then and there to deliver up the thing extorted.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Explanation.<\/i>\u2014The offender is said to be present if he is sufficiently near to put the other person in\r\nfear of instant death, of instant hurt, or of instant wrongful restraint.\r\n
Illustrations.<\/i><\/center><\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(a) A holds Z down, and fraudulently takes Z\u2019s money and jewels from Z\u2019s clothes, without Z\u2019s\r\nconsent. Here A has committed theft, and, in order to the committing of that theft, has voluntarily caused\r\nwrongful restraint to Z. A has therefore committed robbery.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(b) A meets Z on the high road, shows a pistol, and demands Z\u2019s purse. Z, in consequence, surrenders\r\nhis purse. Here A has extorted the purse from Z by putting him in fear of instant hurt, and being at the\r\ntime of committing the extortion in his presence. A has therefore committed robbery.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(c) A meets Z and Z\u2019s child on the high road. A takes the child, and threatens to fling it down a\r\nprecipice, unless Z delivers his purse. Z, in consequence, delivers his purse. Here A has extorted the\r\npurse from Z, by causing Z to be in fear of instant hurt to the child who is there present. A has therefore\r\ncommitted robbery on Z.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(d) A obtains property from Z by saying\u2014\"Your child is in the hands of my gang, and will be put to\r\ndeath unless you send us ten thousand rupees\". This is extortion, and punishable as such; but it is not\r\nrobbery, unless Z is put in fear of the instant death of his child.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) Whoever commits robbery shall be punished with rigorous imprisonment for a term which may\r\nextend to ten years, and shall also be liable to fine; and, if the robbery be committed on the highway\r\nbetween sunset and sunrise, the imprisonment may be extended to fourteen years.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(5) Whoever attempts to commit robbery shall be punished with rigorous imprisonment for a term\r\nwhich may extend to seven years, and shall also be liable to fine.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(6) If any person, in committing or in attempting to commit robbery, voluntarily causes hurt, such\r\nperson, and any other person jointly concerned in committing or attempting to commit such robbery, shall\r\nbe punished with imprisonment for life, or with rigorous imprisonment for a term which may extend to\r\nten years, and shall also be liable to fine.<\/br>
