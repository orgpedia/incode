{"footnote":"<\/br>\t\t","content":"<\/span>(1) Every charge under this Sanhita shall state the offence with which the\r\naccused is charged.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(2) If the law which creates the offence gives it any specific name, the offence may be described in the\r\ncharge by that name only.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(3) If the law which creates the offence does not give it any specific name, so much of the definition of\r\nthe offence must be stated as to give the accused notice of the matter with which he is charged.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(4) The law and section of the law against which the offence is said to have been committed shall be\r\nmentioned in the charge.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(5) The fact that the charge is made is equivalent to a statement that every legal condition required by\r\nlaw to constitute the offence charged was fulfilled in the particular case.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(6) The charge shall be written in the language of the Court.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>(7) If the accused, having been previously convicted of any offence, is liable, by reason of such previous\r\nconviction, to enhanced punishment, or to punishment of a different kind, for a subsequent offence, and it\r\nis intended to prove such previous conviction for the purpose of affecting the punishment which the Court\r\nmay think fit, to award for the subsequent offence, the fact, date and place of the previous conviction shall\r\nbe stated in the charge; and if such statement has been omitted, the Court may add it at any time before\r\nsentence is passed.<\/br>
