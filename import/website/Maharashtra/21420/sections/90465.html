\r\n<\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span><\/span>Illustrations.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(a) A lays sticks and turf over a pit, with the intention of thereby causing death, or with the\r\nknowledge that death is likely to be thereby caused. Z, believing the ground to be firm, treads on it, falls\r\nin and is killed. A has committed the offence of culpable homicide.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(b) A knows Z to be behind a bush. B does not know it. A, intending to cause, or knowing it to be\r\nlikely to cause Z\u2019s death, induces B to fire at the bush. B fires and kills Z. Here B may be guilty of no\r\noffence; but A has committed the offence of culpable homicide.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span><\/span>(c) A, by shooting at a fowl with intent to kill and steal it, kills B, who is behind a bush; A not\r\nknowing that he was there. Here, although A was doing an unlawful act, he was not guilty of culpable homicide, as he did not intend to kill B, or to cause death by doing an act that he knew was likely to cause\r\ndeath.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Explanation 1.\u2014A person who causes bodily injury to another who is labouring under a disorder,\r\ndisease or bodily infirmity, and thereby accelerates the death of that other, shall be deemed to have\r\ncaused his death.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Explanation 2.\u2014Where death is caused by bodily injury, the person who causes such bodily injury\r\nshall be deemed to have caused the death, although by resorting to proper remedies and skilful treatment\r\nthe death might have been prevented.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>Explanation 3.\u2014The causing of the death of a child in the mother\u2019s womb is not homicide. But it\r\nmay amount to culpable homicide to cause the death of a living child, if any part of that child has been\r\nbrought forth, though the child may not have breathed or been completely born.<\/br>
