1<\/sup>[CHAPTER IIIA<\/center>
\r\n
PRE-INSTITUTION MEDIATION AND SETTLEMENT <\/center><\/br>
\r\n<\/span>12A. Pre-Institution Mediation and Settlement.<\/b>--- (1) A suit, which does not contemplate any urgent\r\ninterim relief under this Act, shall not be instituted unless the plaintiff exhausts the remedy of preinstitution mediation in accordance with such manner and procedure as may be prescribed by rules made\r\nby the Central Government.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(2) The Central Government may, by notification, authorise the Authorities constituted under the\r\nLegal Services Authorities Act, 1987 (39 of 1987), for the purposes of pre-institution mediation.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(3) Notwithstanding anything contained in the Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987 (39 of 1987), the\r\nAuthority authorised by the Central Government under sub-section (2)<\/i> shall complete the process of\r\nmediation within a period of three months from the date of application made by the plaintiff under\r\nsub-section (1)<\/i>:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\nProvided that the period of mediation may be extended for a further period of two months with the\r\nconsent of the parties:<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\nProvided further that, the period during which the parties remained occupied with the pre-institution\r\nmediation, such period shall not be computed for the purpose of limitation under the Limitation Act, 1963\r\n(36 of 1963). <\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(4) If the parties to the commercial dispute arrive at a settlement, the same shall be reduced into\r\nwriting and shall be signed by the parties to the dispute and the mediator.<\/br>
\r\n<\/span>\r\n(5) The settlement arrived at under this section shall have the same status and effect as if it is an\r\narbitral award on agreed terms under sub-section (4)<\/i> of section 30 of the Arbitration and Conciliation Act,\r\n1996 (26 of 1996).]<\/br>
